Male Wrestlers

Bron Breakker

Bron Breakker is a standout wrestler due to his combination of impressive athleticism, natural charisma, and strong technical skills. Coming from a legendary wrestling family, he brings a powerful, agile style to the ring that captivates audiences. Bron Breakker’s character can be described as having a “dog” mentality, meaning he’s relentless, gritty, and never backs down from a challenge. His intensity in the ring reflects the "dog" mindset — he's always pushing forward, fighting with heart, and giving everything he has to overcome any obstacle. This "dog" mentality comes through in his hard-hitting style and his no-quit attitude, whether he’s facing bigger opponents or in the toughest situations.

  • 2 NXT Championships
  • 2 WWE Intercontinental Championships
  • 1 NXT Tag Team Championship (with Baron Corbin)
  • 1 NXT Dusty Rhodes Tag Team Classic (2024) (with Baron Corbin)

Dominik Mysterio

Dominik Mysterio has quickly proven himself to be a skilled wrestler thanks to his impressive in-ring ability and natural progression as a performer. He combines his high-flying style, inherited from his legendary father Rey Mysterio, with a newfound edge as a heel. Dominik Mysterio has become incredibly over as a heel in WWE, largely due to his compelling character development and the contrast he brings to his family legacy. As the son of Rey Mysterio, fans initially expected him to follow in his father’s footsteps as a babyface, but his shift to a bratty, arrogant persona has made him one of the most hated characters on the roster. His alliance with Judgment Day has helped amplify his heel status, allowing him to lean into his "entitled" attitude, disrespecting his father and other legends.

  • 2 NXT North American Championships
  • 1 WWE SmackDown Tag Team Championship (with Rey Mysterio)

Jey Uso

Jey Uso is a standout wrestler due to his versatility, athleticism, and natural storytelling ability. As part of the renowned Usos tag team, he has showcased incredible chemistry with his brother Jimmy, but Jey has also proven himself as a compelling singles competitor. Fans rallied behind Jey as he fought against the odds and stood up to his cousin (Roman Reigns), showcasing his resilience and heart. His portrayal as a determined, loyal family member battling for his own identity resonated with the audience, making them root for him. Jey's charisma, passion, and natural ability to evoke emotion in his matches and promos have made him a beloved face, earning him the fans' respect and admiration.

  • 1 WWE Intercontinenetal Championship
  • 4 WWE/WWE Raw Tag Team Championships (3 with Jimmy Uso, 1 with Cody Rhodes)
  • 6 WWE SmackDown Tag Team Championships (5 with Jimmy Uso, 1 with Cody Rhodes)
  • 1 WWE André the Giant Memorial Battle Royal (2021)
  • 1 WWE Men's Royal Rumble (2025)

Female Wrestlers

Liv Morgan

Liv Morgan is a standout wrestler due to her incredible athleticism, versatility, and resilience in the ring. She has an impressive ability to connect with fans, blending her high-energy moves with an authentic personality. Liv Morgan’s hard work and dedication are evident in her impressive journey through WWE. From her early days as part of the Riott Squad to her rise as a solo competitor, Liv has shown an unwavering commitment to improving her skills. She’s always pushing herself, constantly evolving her wrestling style, and taking on new challenges. Whether it’s in the gym, perfecting her in-ring techniques, or working on her character, Liv never takes shortcuts.

  • 1 WWE Crown Jewel Championship (2024)
  • 2 WWE SmackDown Women's/Women's World Championships
  • 3 WWE Women's Tag Team Championships (with Raquel Rodriguez)
  • 1 Ms. Money in the Bank (2022)

Raquel Rodriguez

Raquel Rodriguez is a powerhouse wrestler who brings strength, agility, and intensity to the ring. Her imposing size combined with impressive athleticism allows her to dominate opponents with ease, while her ability to execute high-impact moves adds an extra layer of excitement to her matches. Raquel Rodriguez’s strength goes beyond the physical, as she’s also demonstrated incredible resilience in overcoming a personal health challenge—mast cell activation syndrome (MCAS). MCAS is a condition that causes the body’s mast cells to release excessive amounts of histamine, leading to a range of debilitating symptoms, including fatigue, pain, and allergic reactions. Despite dealing with this condition, Raquel has continued to push forward in her wrestling career, never letting it define her or hold her back.

  • 1 NXT Dusty Rhodes Tag Team Classic (2021) (with Dakota Kai)
  • 1 NXT Women's Championship
  • 4 WWE Women's Tag Team Championships (3 with Liv Morgan, 1 with Aliyah)
  • 2 NXT Women's Tag Team Championships (with Dakota Kai)

Becky Lynch

Becky Lynch is one of the most talented and charismatic wrestlers in WWE, known for her exceptional in-ring ability and captivating persona. Her hard-hitting, technical style combined with her fiery determination has earned her a reputation as one of the best in the business. Becky’s rise to prominence, particularly her transformation into "The Man," showcased her ability to connect with fans on a deep level, The phrase resonated with fans because it was a declaration of equality and empowerment—Becky wasn't just a female wrestler; she was "the" wrestler, regardless of gender. The persona marked her transition from a fan-favorite underdog to a dominant champion.

  • 5 WWE SmackDown Women's/Women's World Championships
  • 2 WWE Raw Women's Championships
  • 1 NXT Women's Championship
  • 1 WWE Women's Tag Team Championship (with Lita)
  • 1 WWE Women's Royal Rumble (2019)

How I got into Watching WWE/Wrestling?

I got into wrestling when I was about 7 years old, and at the time, John Cena was my absolute favorite. I enjoyed it but didn’t really understand what was going on—at that age, I just thought it was real wrestling. Then, I found out that wrestling was scripted, and honestly, I didn’t really care much after that. Fast forward to my mid-to-late teen years, when I came across a clip of Liv Morgan hitting her Oblivion finisher. It shocked me because it looked so real, and I was amazed by the fact that there was a story and dialogue involved. That’s when it clicked for me: WWE was like a reality TV show, but with wrestling added for extra flair. From there, I became instantly hooked and became a loyal fan of The Judgment Day, particularly Liv, Raquel, Dom, and the others. I found myself getting lost in the world of WWE, where I had to use my imagination to truly immerse myself in the characters and storylines. It wasn’t just about the moves in the ring—it was about building that connection to the drama, the rivalries, and the triumphs. On days when I don’t have school or homework, I watch old matches and storylines, diving deeper to understand the plots and make sure I’m not just a "fake" wrestling fan, but a real, dedicated one who can truly appreciate the art of the storytelling.
